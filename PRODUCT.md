# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Tech-adjacent readers: engineering peers, potential collaborators, folks who found Omar through GitHub or Twitter. They visit on a laptop, likely daytime, often between tasks. They want to read something real — not a press release, not a hustle blog.

## Product Purpose

Personal brand site for Omar Martinez: Engineering Lead, DJ, dad. A home for writing about tech and life, a showcase for projects built with vibe coding, and a place that communicates "this is a whole person" rather than a resume. Success looks like someone reading one post and bookmarking the site.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, minimal, confident. The site trusts the content — it doesn't dress it up in decorations. The warmth is in the writing, not the chrome.

## Anti-references

- Generic dev blog (Hashnode/Dev.to templates, interchangeable with thousands of others)
- Overdone dark-mode hacker aesthetic (matrix green, very "programmer brand")
- Minimalist-to-the-point-of-boring (so stripped that it has no personality)

## Design Principles

1. **Trust the content.** The design recedes. Typography and spacing carry the page, not decoration.
2. **Warmth through restraint.** The palette is earthy and warm, but applied sparingly so it feels considered, not cozy.
3. **Confident hierarchy.** Big headings, clear scale, no ambiguity about what to read first.
4. **One identity.** Engineering Lead + DJ + Dad is a single person, not three separate personas. The site holds all of it without compartmentalizing.
5. **No performance.** No metric grids, no social proof widgets, no growth-hacking patterns. Just the work.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Keyboard navigation and reduced motion already implemented. Maintain AA contrast for all text roles (body, secondary, tertiary). Dark mode parity — both themes meet AA.
